Who am I

The engineer, not the title.

I started in the machine room — Solaris, AIX, and petabyte-scale SAN/NAS at a global storage vendor, back when a bad change meant driving to the datacenter at 2am. That decade of fundamentals is why the cloud work holds up: I know exactly what the abstractions are abstracting.

Since then I've built cloud platforms at enterprise scale: I built Shell's Enterprise Cloud Platform from scratch, growing it from a managed service into a self-service platform for 100+ engineering teams, and served as the main architect for every AWS landing zone aspect at Shell — account vending, guardrails, networking, identity, the lot. Landing zones are my specialty, and I've written hundreds of Lambdas to make them run touchless. I pioneered MLOps there — first engineer to put SageMaker and Azure ML into production as shared services, before "MLOps" was even the word people used. I led a FinOps programme that returned €13M in verified savings in twelve months. I rebuilt CI/CD across 600+ AWS accounts and cut deployment cycles from a week to 20 minutes. I sat on the Technical Review Board, judging the exceptions — the cases where the rulebook runs out and someone has to reason from first principles.

I've been the individual contributor, the tech lead, the resource manager, the product owner, the SRE coach, and the agile transformer — sometimes all in the same quarter. I lead by example: I write production Python daily, and on my current engagement I author roughly half the team's commits while leading it. That's also why people follow — engineers I've led and mentored would join anything I start, any day. I took a newly merged, dysfunctional 25-person team and turned it into a high-performing SRE org using SRE principles, Scrum, and logical reasoning about what was actually broken — not a framework applied by rote.

I'm a strong SAFe and Agile practitioner — PI planning, ART coordination, scaled delivery — but I'm a fan of common sense with framework, never framework over common sense.

Right now the most interesting work is autonomous multi-agent AI in production — AWS Bedrock, AgentCore, and Strands SDK — applying the same reliability discipline to agents that SRE brought to services. It's why I started Vawsome: to take that discipline to teams beyond the one I lead day to day.
